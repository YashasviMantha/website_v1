Let me start off by thanking you for making it so far! It means a lot to me. So I am currently pursuing my bachelors in engineering. It all started when I took electronics as my vocational subject in highschool. Where we were taught about how an actual bit is stored (flip/flops) in a computer. It was really hard for me to believe that a flip/flop being the fundamental unit was multiplied and miniaturised to make an oceanic storage. This was when I knew, what I wanted to do with my life. What I wanted to spend most of my time on. I actually ended up making a smart door look system with a few shields and sensors I brought of on Flipkart. This was when I had around zero knowledge on programming if not in negative. But I managed to put Arduino (a popular microcontroller) and some other shields together. I was proud if it.

I then ended up picking computer science as my major which was when I was introduced to C programming for the first time in 2017. Then there was no looking back. I almost put my hand everywhere, I explored every possible domain. I developed a few games in Unity. I worked on cryptography. I started competitive programming and whatnot. But nothing clicked as data science and genetics did. I still remember my first project in which I automated the entire process of logging into my college portal like it was yesterday. I used selenium and tesseract for solving captcha which is an entirely different story of its own.

I spent most of my first year exploring. But when I became a senior, I invested time seriously into data science. I got the opportunity to intern in one of the leading NLP labs in India under Diptesh Kanojia and Professor Pushpak Bhattacharyya at IIT Bombay. Got my first research paper published and made some great friends at CFILT. I was also interested in how college fests were conducted which is why I wanted to work in a student body. I actively took part in my college's local ACM student chapter where we organised a 15-day fest. This included a special event of the famous standup comedian Sorabh Pant where I and a fellow senior of mine were the core managers of the entire setup. I ended up being the Joint Secretary of the student body this year, so kudos to that.
